01 Quarterly Sales Report

Built the Q3 2024 (Oct–Dec) sales report for two sub-categories, Sweets Ambient and Sweets Frozen, tracking performance across volume (MT) and value (₹ Crore). The report covered state, channel and product segment breakdowns comparing current-year against last-year across 12M, 6M, 3M and monthly windows.

The key challenge was interpreting a ~70% sales dip in November. By overlapping the festival calendar against sales data, I identified that the Diwali buying window had shifted from November to October that year, confirming that growth remained consistent when properly normalised. I also flagged that December volume growth was outpacing value due to heavy end-of-season discounting, and traced an extreme value/volume divergence in one state to prior-year excess inventory clearance.

02 New SKU Feasibility & Approval

Led the feasibility assessment for launching a new grammage variant of an existing Soan Papdi product, targeting the ₹5 impulse-purchase segment where competitors had SKUs but Haldiram did not.

I built a financial model mapping the existing product's cost structure including GRM, MRP, PTR%, PTSS%, GST% and gross contribution per kg, then rebuilt it for the proposed grammage with revised pricing. The side-by-side comparison demonstrated the new SKU could achieve positive gross contribution while filling the identified market gap, and the output was presented as a structured feasibility case for internal approval.

Existing pricing — PTD, PTSS, and gross contribution per kg across current Soan Papdi variants
Proposed SKU — revised grammage, PTD/PTSS structure, and projected gross contribution
03 Competitive Price Benchmarking

Conducted structured price benchmarking across Haldiram's Soan Papdi and Tin Sweets range, comparing GMS prices against key competitors including HR Current/New, BIKAJI, LAL and SATMOLA, mapping price per grammage tier across pack sizes and assessing margin implications.

In one top-performing sub-category, the analysis showed that a recent price hike was driving value growth ahead of volume. Through competitor benchmarking I identified that customers were migrating to lower-priced competitor SKUs, pointing to demand contraction from price sensitivity rather than genuine pricing strength. This directly informed the recommendation to hold price in that segment and fed into both the SKU feasibility case and the quarterly report.
