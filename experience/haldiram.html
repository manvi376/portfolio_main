01 Quarterly Sales Report

Built the Q3 2024 (Oct–Dec) sales report for two sub-categories — Sweets Ambient and Sweets Frozen — tracking performance across volume (MT) and value (₹ Crore). The analysis covered state, channel, and product segment breakdowns, comparing current-year vs. last-year across 12M, 6M, 3M, and monthly windows.

The main challenge was interpreting a ~70% sales dip in November. I traced it to a shift in the Diwali festival calendar — the peak buying period had moved from November (LY) to October (CY). Overlapping the festival dates against sales data confirmed that growth remained consistent when properly normalised. A secondary finding: December's volume growth was outpacing value growth because of heavy end-of-season discounting. One state showed an extreme value/volume divergence — traced through EDA to excess inventory clearance from prior-year over-stocking.

02 New SKU Feasibility & Approval

Developed a financial model to assess the feasibility of launching a new grammage variant of an existing Soan Papdi product, targeting the underserved ₹5 impulse-purchase segment where competitors had SKUs but Haldiram did not.

The model mapped the existing product's cost structure (GRM, MRP, PTR%, PTSS%, GST%, gross contribution per kg) then rebuilt it for the proposed grammage with revised pricing. The side-by-side comparison showed the new SKU could achieve positive gross contribution while filling the market gap — the output was a structured feasibility case presented for internal approval.

03 Competitive Price Benchmarking

Conducted structured price benchmarking across Haldiram's Soan Papdi and Tin Sweets range, comparing GMS prices against HR Current/New, BIKAJI, LAL, and SATMOLA — mapping price per grammage tier across pack sizes and assessing margin implications.

In one top-performing sub-category, the analysis revealed that a recent price hike was driving value growth ahead of volume — indicating demand contraction from price sensitivity rather than pricing strength. Customers were migrating to competitor SKUs at lower price points. This finding directly informed the recommendation to hold price in that segment, and fed into both the SKU feasibility case and the quarterly report's interpretation of the value/volume divergence.
